Urs Berger Biography

I was born in Basel Switzerland. My father worked as a postmaster and my mother was a housemaker. I have four brothers, I am number 4. I studied biology at the University of Basel. I received a Ph.D. in cellular biology from the University of Basel for thesis work that I had carried out in the Department of Neuroscience of the Johns Hopkings University Medical Center in Baltimore, MD. I had postdoctoral positions at the New York University, and the Mass General and Brigham and Women's Hospitals in Boston. I spent a year supervising a core facility that specializes in performing In Situ Hybridization for the detection of messenger RNAs at the Beth Israel Deaconess Medical Center in Boston. Subsequently I carried out similar work as an independent consultant through my company UB-In Situ. In 2011, I became in interested in Web programming and design, as well as programming. I have taken a few computer science classes at Tufts University since then. I married in 1992 and live with my wife and a 16 year old daughter, and a 12 year old son in Massachussetts.
